# venture-capital-dashboard

## Project Overview

This project simulates the work of a Data Science Intern supporting data strategy, data governance, and business intelligence initiatives within a private equity or investment organization.

The project focuses on conducting data quality assessments, documenting business data, integrating multiple startup investment datasets, and developing executive reporting dashboards to support investment analysis and decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

Investment firms collect information from multiple sources, including venture capital investments, funding rounds, acquisitions, and IPO events. When this data is stored across separate systems, it becomes difficult to monitor investment activity, analyze market trends, and evaluate startup exit outcomes.

The goal of this project is to create a centralized analytics solution that transforms raw startup ecosystem data into actionable business insights.

---

Source: Crunchbase Startup Investments Dataset

Datasets used:

* acquisitions.csv
* funds.csv
* investments.csv
* funding_rounds.csv
* ipos.csv
* objects.csv

---

## Project Objectives

* Conduct a data quality audit to identify missing values, inconsistencies, and data integrity issues.
* Create a data dictionary documenting important business fields and definitions.
* Build an integrated analytics dataset by connecting investment, fund, acquisition, IPO, and company information.
* Develop SQL queries that support business reporting and KPI generation.
* Create an executive Tableau dashboard to visualize investment activity and exit performance.
* Demonstrate version control and project organization through GitHub workflows.


---

## Key Business Questions

### 1. How has startup funding activity changed over time?

### 2. Which venture capital funds have raised the most capital?

### 3. Who are the most active investors?

### 4. How do startups most commonly exit: Acquisition or IPO?

### 5. What acquisition and IPO events generated the highest valuations?
